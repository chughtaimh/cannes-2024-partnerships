OMG and GroupM Win Day One of Cannes Partnerships

June 18, 2024 - 5 min read

At the Cannes Lions Festival, the first day saw major partnership wins for OMG and GroupM.

GroupM’s Shopping Smarts

GroupM, WPP’s media giant, announced a collaboration with measurement company Incremental, promising to improve how retail media buys are evaluated. With AI-driven planning tools, forecasting, measurement, and analytics integrated into GroupM’s Open Media Studio, clients can seamlessly manage retail media alongside other digital channels. Samantha Bukowski, GroupM’s global head of commerce, emphasized the need for rigorous planning across all digital dollars.

“We want to be able to plan our retail media investment dollars in the same place, with the same rigor, as we’re planning the rest of our digital dollars,” said Samantha Bukowski, GroupM’s global head of commerce.

In another strategic move, GroupM partnered with Shalion, a retail intelligence leader. Integrating Shalion’s advanced analytics into Open Media Studio, this partnership enhances insights across 18 markets and more than 5,000 retailer and category combinations. Bukowski called it a milestone in bringing retail intelligence to the heart of marketing processes and technologies.


Finally, GroupM connected the dots between shopper audiences and video content by partnering with Amazon to deliver shoppable video moments to viewers. While shoppable television advertising is nothing new (many streamers, including Disney and NBCU have announced these capabilities in the past few months), this partnership is unique in that it seems Amazon will develop custom, original shoppable content for the Amazon FAST channel for brands like Danone.

“These shows … offer brands across all industries a powerful way to connect with audiences and drive results,” said Julie Haleluk, global head of growth for Amazon Shopping Videos, in a statement.

Omnicom Develops Unique Platform Partnerships

Not to be outdone, Omnicom announced a first-of-its-kind partnership with The Trade Desk, developing custom investment solutions for Omnicom clients. This initiative integrates The Trade Desk’s extensive TV data with Flywheel’s commerce data through Omni, Omnicom’s operating system. (Omnicom acquired digital commerce firm Flywheel last year.)

“We have engineers working on their side, and on our side… to create bespoke solutions for our clients,” explained Megan Pagliuca, Chief Activation Officer at Omnicom Media Group. “We’re optimizing The Trade Desk data, bringing in Flywheel commerce cloud signals, and then optimizing based on sales. That’s a big deal in terms of how The Trade Desk has changed their engagement to create bespoke solutions.”

The Trade Desk alone offers 3 trillion streaming impressions per month, according to Omnicom.


Omnicom also teamed up with Amazon Ads to connect audience data to browsing and shopping insights. This partnership allows Omnicom’s teams to access Amazon’s proprietary data, enabling more efficient investment decisions and connecting TV and CTV investments directly to sales on Amazon. Clarissa Season, chief experience officer at Omnicom’s Annalect, explained the ecosystem that connects viewership data to audience data to Amazon sales data.

“We‘ve created an ecosystem that allows us to connect viewership data to audience data to the Amazon sales data, with the addition of Flywheel,” said Clarissa Season, chief experience officer at Omnicom’s Annalect, the minds behind Omni.

These are pretty innovative platform partnerships for a big holding company to develop, and shows how serious Omnicom is at developing a platform edge over its rivals.

Proving the Value of Media Investments

A central theme emerging from these partnerships is the focus on incrementality. Unlike traditional ROAS, incrementality narrows down to sales that wouldn’t have occurred without the ad campaign. David Pollet, CEO of Incremental, explained that incrementality requires understanding where ads made a difference, aiming to cut out sales already in motion.


Day one of the Cannes Lions Festival has already set a high bar with these strategic partnerships from OMG and GroupM. By embracing advanced measurement techniques, integrating retail intelligence, and pioneering shoppable content, these agencies are addressing current challenges and paving the way for the future of digital advertising. As the festival continues, the industry will be watching closely to see how these innovations unfold and transform the advertising landscape.
